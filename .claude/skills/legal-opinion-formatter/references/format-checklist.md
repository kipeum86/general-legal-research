# Legal Opinion Format Checklist

## Document Architecture

1. Title and confidentiality marker
2. Client / matter / as-of date
3. Scope and assumptions
4. Short answer / bottom line
5. Issue-by-issue detailed reasoning
6. Risk grade and recommended actions
7. Source table (with pinpoints)
8. Verification / limitations / disclaimer

## Quality Expectations

- Headings are consistent and numbered.
- Tables have fixed columns and readable widths.
- Paragraph spacing and line spacing are uniform.
- Korean/English legal terms are paired where useful.
- Every key conclusion maps to at least one source citation.

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Mixing policy suggestions with legal conclusions without separation.
- Ambiguous "maybe/possibly" statements without uncertainty tags.
- Long unstructured paragraphs without issue headings.
- Citation codes that do not appear in source list.

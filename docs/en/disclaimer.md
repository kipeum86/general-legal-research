## AI Limitations - Hallucinations and Errors

This project is powered by a large language model (Claude). LLMs can and do produce **hallucinations**, **factual errors**, and **incorrect legal analysis**. Outputs may:

- **Fabricate legal authorities** by citing statutes, regulations, case law, or agency guidance that do not exist
- **Misstate the law** by presenting an outdated, repealed, or incorrectly interpreted rule as current and valid
- **Conflate jurisdictions** by attributing a legal rule to the wrong country, state, or regulatory body
- **Omit material issues** by failing to identify relevant regulations, exceptions, or pending legislative changes
- **Misclassify source hierarchy** by treating non-binding guidance, blog commentary, or secondary summaries as if they were primary legal authority
- **Invent citations** by generating plausible-looking but entirely fictitious case names, docket numbers, statute references, or article titles

The agent includes built-in safeguards such as Step 4 fact-checking, source grading, quality gates, conflict detection, and `[Unverified]` tagging, but **these safeguards reduce, not eliminate, the risk of error**. A qualified human must always review and verify every output before any reliance or action. The agent assists; the human decides.

## Data Handling

The agent makes LLM API calls, which means research queries and collected source text are sent to the model provider (Anthropic). No data is stored on external servers beyond the API request lifecycle under standard commercial terms.

If you use this tool for matters involving confidential or privileged information:
